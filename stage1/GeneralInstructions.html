This experiment will take place on two separate days—today, and the same day in four weeks’ time. Please only take part in today’s session if you are able to attend the session in four weeks. You will receive a $5 show-up fee today, and an additional $45 if you also successfully complete the session in four weeks.

Today's experimental session will have two sections: a “choice” section and a “work” section. The session in four weeks will consist only of a “work” section.

Each work section will last for 45 minutes. During this time, you will complete simple tasks on your computer. Specifically, you will be prompted at random times to answer simple questions (e.g., press a particular letter or solve a basic math problem). You will have a short time (5 seconds) to respond.

To successfully complete a work section, you must maintain a minimum accuracy of 80% on these tasks. You will receive the $45 bonus payment only if you successfully complete each work section.

You will have the opportunity to use experimental monetary units (EMUs) to reduce the amount of time you spend performing these tasks (though you will still need to remain in the lab for the full 45 minutes).

Each EMU can be used to reduce your working time by 2 minutes. For example, if you have 3.5 EMUs to spend in a session, you can reduce your working time by 7 minutes (to 38 minutes). During the remaining 7 minutes, you may relax, use your phone, etc. Note that EMUs have no other use besides reducing work time.

The number of EMUs you have for each work section will be determined by your choices in today’s “choice” section.

In this section, you will be asked to choose: (i) how many EMUs to use today and how many to save for four weeks later, and (ii) which of two assets to invest your EMUs in. These assets differ in their probability of paying out and their rate of return.

We will explain the details on the next page. For now, you only need to remember that your decisions in the choice section determine how many EMUs you receive today and in four weeks, which in turn determines how much you need to work in each session.
